<h1>
								IN CASE YOU MISSED IT: 5 key stories from February 14
							</h1>
</header>
<div>
<p class="lead">
								Here are five Fastmarkets MB stories you might have missed on Thursday February 14 that are worth another look.
							</p>
</div>
<span id="article_daArticle"><span>
<div class="articleContainer">
									The Singapore Exchange said its new high-grade iron ore futures, which are settled against the Fastmarkets MB 65% Fe Iron Ore Index, have seen <a href="https://www.metalbulletin.com/Article/3858282/Search-results/Iron-ore-trading-landscape-changing-with-high-grade-derivatives-launch-SGX-says.html " target="_blank">over 3 million tonnes worth of transactions</a> since its December 3 launch involving 30 market participants. <br/>
<br/>
There are indications that China <a href="https://www.metalbulletin.com/Article/3858317/Search-results/COMMENT-Has-China-started-to-import-pig-iron-again.html" target="_blank">might have become the biggest importer of pig iron</a> in Asia last year, according to Fastmarkets’ research team, although detailed data for the East Asian country's trade is still not available.<br/>
<br/>
Fastmarkets AMM summarizes <a href="https://www.metalbulletin.com/Article/3858500/Home/MEXICAN-STEEL-Five-more-things-we-heard.html" target="_blank">five more things learned</a> at the 2019 Mexican Steel Forum held February 12-15 in Monterrey, Mexico. <br/>
<br/>
The suspension of cobalt production at the Chambishi refinery in Zambia <a href="https://www.metalbulletin.com/Article/3858269/Search-results/Chambishi-suspension-offers-short-term-relief-to-cobalt-price-slumpsources.html " target="_blank">could provide some respite to falling cobalt prices</a> but it should not be confused with the eventual departure of Chambishi broken cathodes from the market, according to sources spoken to by Fastmarkets. The <a href="https://www.metalbulletin.com/Article/3858286/Search-results/ERG-suspends-copper-cobalt-production-at-Chambishi.html " target="_blank">suspension was triggered by</a> constrained supply of imported feedstock. <br/>
<br/>
The Alliance for American Manufacturing wants United States lawmakers to <a href="https://www.metalbulletin.com/Article/3858499/Home/AAM-urges-Congress-to-reject-anti-Section-232-legislation.html " target="_blank">reject the recently introduced</a> Bicameral Congressional Trade Authority Act of 2019, arguing that it threatens steel and aluminium sector jobs and investment. <br/>
<br/>
</div>
</span></span>
</div>
